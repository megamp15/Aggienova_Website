AGGIENOVA

ABOUT

The AGGIENOVA research team studies exploding stars (supernovae) using data obtained by ground and space-based observatories, including NASAâ€™s Swift and Hubble space telescopes.

This team will use and build analysis and visualization tools for the Swift Optical Ultraviolet Supernova Archive (SOUSA) with python as the primary language.

Dr. Brown

Project Leader
  • I am a Research Scientist at the Mitchell Institute for Fundamental Physics and Astronomy at Texas A&M University.
  • I lead a NASA-funded project to create the Swift Optical Ultraviolet Supernovae Archive - a UV database of the 600+ Supernovae observed by Swift.
  • I enjoy teaching and mentoring students, and I lead the Aggienova research team through Texas A&M's Aggie Research Program to give more undergraduates a chance to make a real contribution to this exciting research.
